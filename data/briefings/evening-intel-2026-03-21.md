## AI + Industry Pulse

The dominant theme across the industry right now is the shift from AI experimentation to deployment at scale, and the data is starting to back it up. Over 173 AI-discovered drug programs are now in clinical development as of 2026, up from just 3 in 2016 — a figure suggesting AI's ability to select better drug candidates could translate into more efficient development with fewer early failures. Phase I success rates for AI-discovered molecules are running at 81% versus 52% for traditional methods, and Phase II success is at 68% versus 30-45% traditionally — the first statistically credible numbers the industry has had to anchor AI's clinical ROI story.

On the regulatory front, the picture is crystallizing. The FDA's draft AI guidance is expected to be finalized in 2026, requiring sponsors to develop credibility assessment plans for high-risk AI applications and submit detailed documentation on model architectures, training data, and governance. The EU AI Act's high-risk provisions take effect on August 2, 2026, potentially classifying some drug development AI as high-risk. This dual regulatory timeline is forcing pharma companies to build compliance infrastructure now — a direct tailwind for AWS's data governance and compliance positioning.

On funding, the AI-drug discovery startup market is white-hot with public market validation. Eikon Therapeutics and Generate:Biomedicines both completed IPOs in February 2026, raising $381 million and $400 million respectively, pointing to a healthy public-market window for AI-native biotech. The year kicked off with a stream of AI platform deals across pharma, including collaborations between Chai Discovery and Eli Lilly, Noetik and GSK, and Boltz and Pfizer — signaling a cultural shift away from single-asset bets and toward investment in AI infrastructure for broad discovery.

The agentic AI theme is specifically heating up in life sciences. NVIDIA's Kimberly Powell noted the $4.9 trillion healthcare industry is deploying AI at more than twice the rate of the broader economy, and the startup ecosystem is driving this shift, capturing over 85% of healthcare AI spending last year. Industry experts say 2026 will mark the year digital twins move from pilot to practice in clinical development, with sponsors increasingly exploring them to optimize protocol design, reduce amendments, and accelerate timelines.
